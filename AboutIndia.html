India
India is an incredible tapestry of cultures, languages, traditions, and history. It's the world's largest democracy, with over 1.4 billion people. Geographically, India is diverse, stretching from the towering Himalayas in the north to the tropical beaches in the south, encompassing deserts, plains, forests, and more. It's bounded by the Indian Ocean, the Arabian Sea, and the Bay of Bengal, giving it a vast coastline.

How does India look on the world map?

India is a large and distinctively shaped country on the world map, often described as resembling a diamond or a teardrop hanging from the base of Asia. It is located in South Asia and is bordered by the Indian Ocean to the south, making its peninsular part quite prominent. To the west, it shares borders with Pakistan; to the north, it touches China, Nepal, and Bhutan; and to the east, it neighbors Bangladesh and Myanmar. India's geographical features include the towering Himalayas to the north, the expansive Indo-Gangetic plain, and the coastal areas flanking the Arabian Sea and the Bay of Bengal. History of India

Indian history is a fascinating and vast tapestry that stretches back thousands of years, marked by rich cultural, religious, and political evolutions. Here's an overview of its key phases:

Ancient India(c. 1500 BCE – 600 CE)

  • Valley Civilization (c. 2500–1900 BCE): One of the world's earliest urban civilizations, known for its advanced city planning, trade, and water systems. Major cities like Mohenjo-Daro and Harappa were part of this era.

    Mohenjo-Daro


  • Vedic Period (c. 1500–500 BCE): This era saw the composition of the Vedas, foundational texts of Hinduism, and the rise of early kingdoms and social structures.

    Vedic-Civilization


  • Maurya Empire (321–185 BCE): Under Emperor Ashoka, India witnessed the spread of Buddhism and an era of great administration and infrastructure.

    Emperor Ashok


  • Gupta Empire (c. 320–550 CE): Often referred to as the "Golden Age" of India, this era brought advancements in science, mathematics (invention of zero!), art, and literature.

    Gupta Empire

    • Early Medieval India (700-1200 CE):

    Medieval Indian history, generally spanning from the 7th to the 18th centuries, is a rich period marked by the rise and fall of various kingdoms, the emergence of new religious and cultural movements, and the arrival of foreign powers. It can be broadly divided into the early and late medieval periods, each with distinct characteristics. The early medieval period saw the fragmentation of large empires into smaller regional kingdoms, while the late medieval period saw the consolidation of power under the Delhi Sultanate and the Mughal Empire.

  • Rise of Regional Kingdoms: Following the decline of the Gupta Empire, India fragmented into numerous regional kingdoms, including the Pratiharas, Palas, and Rashtrakutas in the north, and the Cholas, Pallavas, and Rashtrakutas in the south.

    • Rise of Regional Kingdoms

  • Tripartite Struggle: A major feature of this period was the "tripartite struggle" between the Pratiharas, Palas, and Rashtrakutas for control of Kannauj (modern-day Uttar Pradesh).
